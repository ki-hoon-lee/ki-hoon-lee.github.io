Overview

Structural Stress Analysis of a Door Coat Hanger

This project applied Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to evaluate the structural integrity of a door-mounted coat hanger under loading conditions. Using Autodesk Nastran, a shell element model was constructed and subjected to distributed loads representative of real-world use, producing a full stress contour map across the component geometry.

The analysis focused on identifying critical stress concentrations, evaluating deformation behaviour, and assessing whether the design met acceptable structural safety margins — a fundamental workflow in modern mechanical design validation.

Analysis & Results

Stress Distribution & Key Findings

The Von Mises stress contour plots reveal the full stress distribution across the hanger geometry. The maximum stress of 245.1 MPa occurs at the inner radius of the hook bend — a predictable stress concentration point where the geometry transitions sharply and bending moments peak.

The base of the hanger and straight sections remain in the low-stress blue range, confirming that material is used efficiently across most of the component. The deformed shape overlay (dashed red) illustrates the deflection pattern under load, with maximum total displacement of 3.71 mm at the hook tip.

Three views were produced — full geometry, upper hook region, and lower curved section — allowing stress gradients to be examined at multiple scales for a thorough structural assessment.
