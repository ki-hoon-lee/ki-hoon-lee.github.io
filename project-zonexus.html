Designing the Next Generation of Electron Microscopy Sample Holders

ZoNexus designs precision instruments for transmission electron microscopy (TEM) and scanning electron microscopy (SEM) — enabling researchers to study materials at the atomic scale. As Mechanical Design Engineer, I own the full product lifecycle for multiple hardware platforms: from concept and CAD through tolerance analysis, fabrication, and assembly validation.

My work spans four product lines, each solving a distinct challenge in sample preparation and in-situ experimentation. Across all products I work within tight vacuum-compatibility, cryogenic, and electromechanical constraints — producing GD&T-compliant drawings and collaborating closely with Lawrence Berkeley National Lab on rapid iteration cycles.

Product 01

Continuous Rotation Tomography Holder

ZoNexus continuous rotation TEM tomography holder — 360° piezo-motor driven rotation

Conventional tomography holders rely on the microscope's goniometer for rotation, which limits the tilt angle to ~70° — resulting in a "missing wedge" artifact that degrades tomogram quality. ZoNexus solved this with a holder featuring a built-in high-precision piezo-motor enabling full 360° continuous rotation, eliminating the missing wedge entirely.

Product Features

The holder supports both continuous rotation mode (for fast acquisition with direct electron detectors) and step-and-collect mode (compatible with standard goniometer tilt series software). Detachable tips allow FIB-prepared samples to be transferred directly. Three tip types are included: needle, half-grid, and full-grid.

My Contribution

I added a biasing feature to the holder tip, enabling users to load and electrically bias a MEMS chip at the end of the holder for in-situ electrical measurements during tomographic imaging — expanding the holder's capability beyond pure structural characterisation.

Product 02

High Throughput Air-Free Transfer Holder

ZoNexus high throughput air-free TEM holder — 3-sample capacity with clamshell loading design

Air-sensitive materials (battery electrodes, catalysts, reactive metals) degrade within seconds of exposure to atmosphere. This TEM holder solves that by allowing 3 samples to be loaded inside a glove box and transferred directly to the TEM without any air exposure — using a user-friendly clamshell-type enclosure that simplifies loading in confined glove-box environments.

My Contribution

I designed and implemented a stepper motor-controlled positioning system for the sample stage, allowing the user to precisely select and position individual samples programmatically — enabling automated, reproducible sample switching without breaking the vacuum seal or exposing to air. The system supports both manual and motor-driven control modes.

Product 03

Peltier Cold Stage for SEM/FIB

ZoNexus Peltier cold stage for SEM/FIB — enabling cryo-temperature experiments inside the SEM chamber

Cryo-electron microscopy requires precise, stable temperature control inside the SEM vacuum chamber. This Peltier-based cold stage enables researchers to perform low-temperature in-situ experiments directly within the SEM/FIB — cooling samples for cryo-preservation, condensation studies, and temperature-dependent materials characterisation.

My Contribution

I developed a PID temperature controller for the Peltier stage — designing the control loop logic, tuning the PID gains for stable setpoint tracking, and integrating the controller with the stage hardware. The system achieves accurate, stable temperature control across the operating range required for cryo-SEM applications.

Product 04

Air-Free Transfer Module for SEM/FIB

ZoNexus air-free transfer module — US Patent US2021/03571A1

This patented module (US Patent US2021/03571A1) consists of a small chamber mountable on the SEM stage. It can be loaded with air-sensitive samples in a glove box, sealed, and transported to the SEM/FIB without any atmosphere exposure. After analysis or FIB liftout, the chamber can be resealed in argon or vacuum for return transfer — enabling a fully closed workflow from glove box to SEM to TEM.

Salient Features

Air-free (inert gas) or vacuum transfer between glove box and SEM/FIB. The system includes the transfer module, a touchscreen controller, and a remote controller for glove-box operation. Compatible with ZoNexus air-free TEM holders for seamless cross-instrument sample workflows.

My Contribution

I added a biasing stub option to the SEM module — enabling in-situ electrical biasing experiments within the SEM chamber without compromising the air-free seal. This extends the module's capability to include electrical characterisation alongside standard imaging and FIB workflows.
